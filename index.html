I.

The Beginning

I knew her my whole life before I really knew her. She was three years older than me, always just there, like part of how things were. Then one day she fell asleep in the other room, and something in me got up and walked in without asking my own permission. I sat down next to her. I took her hand, soft, careful, like it might wake her, like it might break. I kissed it. Not once. I lost count. I was fifteen. She was eighteen. I didn't understand what was happening to me. I just knew I was crying, quietly, and brushing her hair back from her face, even though I'd never done that before. That's the night my hands knew before my mind did. That's when loving her stopped being something I'd just always done, and turned into something I actually felt, sitting in my chest, real and a little scary.

II.

The Small Stuff

She came over to my house with her parents once, back when she was maybe fourteen or fifteen and I was twelve. We were just messing around, the way kids do, and somehow she ended up kicking me right in the stomach. Not on purpose. Just one of those things that happens when you're not paying attention to how hard you're playing.

I went straight to the bathroom and cried. Not even because it hurt that bad. I think twelve-year-old me just didn't know what else to do with it.

Years later, in February, when I was seventeen, she came over again. That's when we kissed for the first time. She told me she didn't know how to kiss. Then I kissed her anyway, and she couldn't even breathe right after. We still laugh about that one, the two of us, every time it comes up.

She probably doesn't even remember the kick. I still do. I think about how she had no idea, back then, that I'd spend years carrying around a memory of her without her ever knowing it was there.

III.

The Hard Season

I told her I loved her for the first time when I was seventeen, away at hostel. It was the first time I'd ever said those words to her out loud. She didn't say it back right away, but she didn't turn me away either. For a while it felt like something was finally starting.

Then she went quiet for ten days. I called all day, every day, and she never picked up. I didn't understand why. Eventually we found our way back to talking.

Later, she stopped texting me for six months. Some of that was on me. I was insecure, I still am sometimes, and I did things back then I'm not proud of, things that shouldn't have happened. I was blocked everywhere I could reach her, so I wrote her emails instead, then asked my sister to pass on a message when I couldn't even do that.

She forgave me anyway. I don't think I've ever properly told her how much that meant.

We got through both of those, somehow. That's still true now.

IV.

Where We Are Now

This isn't the first time it's gone quiet between us. It probably won't be the last. I've learned that about us we go silent, and then somehow we always find our way back.

Right now it's quiet again. I won't pretend otherwise. But I've stopped being scared of the silence the way I used to be, because every time before, you came back. And so did I. That pattern means something to me, even when it's hard.

V.

Where We're Going

I don't want a small version of this. I want to marry you, properly, for real, for the rest of my life. I want to be the reason you laugh more than you used to. I want to give you the kind of love I never got, so you never have to wonder if you're loved enough.

I think about you as the mother of my kids someday. I think about giving you everything I have, not because I have to, but because I want to spend my whole life trying.

I know things are hard right now. That doesn't change any of this. This is still where I want us to go, whenever we get there.
